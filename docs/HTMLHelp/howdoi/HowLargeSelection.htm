How Do I Make a Large Selection?

 

Making a small selection is accomplished easily by dragging with the mouse.  If the area to be selected is larger than one screenful you can still select it by dragging to the top or bottom of the window and waiting for the window to "autoscroll".  HexEdit 2.1 introduced "autoscroll acceleration"  which means that you can control the speed of the scroll by how far you drag the mouse from the edge of the window.

 

But if you want to make a large selection, of many Kbytes or even Mbytes then it can take a long time to drag out the selection even at the fastest autoscroll speed.  In this case you can click on the first byte of the selection then use the vertical scrollbar to scroll to the end of the selection and then Shift-click (hold down the Shift key while clicking the left mouse button) to mark the end of the selection.

 

When making a large selection in a huge file (of Gigabytes or Terabytes in size) even this method has problems due to the scrollbar resolution.  See How Do I Make a Large Selection in a Huge File? for how to get around this problem.
